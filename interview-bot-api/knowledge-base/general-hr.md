## HR — What Is Your Greatest Weakness?

> Sometimes I jumped into development too quickly instead of asking enough questions upfront. I have actively worked on this.

**The honest answer**

Earlier in my career, when I received the basic requirements for a feature, I would **start building immediately** to move things forward quickly. There were a few situations where I had already completed a large part of the implementation, but later the **product priorities changed** or the project was deprioritised, which meant the work didn't end up being used.

**What I changed**

That experience helped me realise the importance of **clarifying requirements, priorities, and long-term goals before investing significant development time**. Over the past few years, I have consciously improved this by:

- **Asking more questions** during planning discussions.
- **Validating assumptions** with product managers.
- **Confirming priorities** before starting larger implementations.

## HR — Do You Have Any Questions for Us?

> Five questions I always ask. They show I am evaluating fit, not just hoping to get hired.

**My go-to questions**

1. What does **success look like** in this role after 6 months?
2. What is the **biggest technical challenge** the team faces right now?
3. How does the team **handle technical debt**?
4. What does **career growth** look like for this role?
5. How is the **engineering team structured**, and how do you collaborate with product?
